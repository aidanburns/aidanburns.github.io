WHIRLWIND

a soft breeze brushes over the rose petals
in the reflecting pool outside the old house.
she’s playing the harp next to the ocean,
on the balcony, the notes glide atop the waves
and slip silently into the water, fluttering
in the sun, they drift deeper, folding away
like thin sheets of origami paper, they glow,
burn up, turn translucent. she threw the sheets
onto the ground outside the church, etched
a smiley-face into the snow with her heel.
she thought about quitting. she thought about
her syllabus, climbing back into the tar pit
of nostalgia that lead her here, wasting time,
watching clocks gradually reverse, living
backwards, remembering littleroot and juniper,
falling asleep in her childhood wardrobe. here
she would begin to dream again, learn to float
weightlessly and live between places, live
in silent free fall through a life worth forgetting.
years ago, they left a chalk outline on her bedroom
floor, a ghost deep-set within. the sun broke
through the crumbling walls and into the darkened room,
the vapour shook its head, yearned to stand,
crafted form from comatose delirium. she
shielded her eyes for a moment, and slowly unfurled
out of the ground, finger by finger,
limping out of the house and reaching upright
over the reflecting pool. placing her hands
into the cold water, the haze of her skin reformed,
wove memory into flesh, and in the water
she held her own reflection, still transparent
in the half-space, and spoke between selves;
“riley, are you there?”
