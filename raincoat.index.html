RAINCOAT

at the foot of the mountain
my friend and I played,
climbed trees and ran circles
around each other until we were dizzy

we started a race
once to the oak tree and back
she started strong, but i pulled ahead,
grinning as i went
through the snow
over a log
and through the air

i held out my arms to break the fall
the snow was kind enough to support me
shaking my head
and i looked up
and my friend was nowhere to be seen

after looking for the longest time
i saw something up ahead
a cave, leading deep into the mountain

i pulled out my torch and headed inside
the darkness was a cold blanket
the light cut through,
the cave giving out to a vast chasm
lined with balconies and laughter
and a stained-glass heart, bright red,
floating a few metres off the ground

i saw it speak,
everything turned blue,
i saw my friend with open arms,
waiting for me

i took her hand,
and we floated,
up through all the noise
