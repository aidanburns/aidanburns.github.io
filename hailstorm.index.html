HAILSTORM

but the reflection was different every time
she looked through the shimmering window
and out into the storm; every shard cut through
the clouds of fog with force, she saw knives dance
and glint with gold, whales pulse with breath,
lurching their ribs outwards like fluttering bugs,
glacial water pooling in an infinite cavern, a ripple
and a whisper. that cold fell deep beneath under her skin,
it flowed through her nerves before diving lower
and setting in a web, pressing her shoulders down
with an eventual exhale. she tilted her head,
growing a smile with wide eyes, and a hand
reached out into the storm. gradually, the shaking
would start, beating cuts into the soft dirt,
the air buzzing with electric shocks. she dug her fingers in,
pushed her weather-battered head upright
with eyes clenched shut, and tried to feel
like nothing was there. holding her arms out,
each step collapsed canyons in her gut,
forming brutal columns of rock, melting like hair in rain,
the waves of sand rolling over her face.
she felt the foundations. there wasn’t space inside,
she couldn’t take the cold. standing at the window,
she balanced atop the knife edge, watching mirrored
half-movements beneath the water, flailing dances
in the open air. the form steadied her breath,
span thoughts into soft shapes, splitting and holding tight,
drifting in a double helix through the warmth of the trick,
until she’s crushed under the weight of the hail,
just hearing a soft voice, echoing inside and out,
‘hazel, are you coming in?’
