THE HOUSE BY THE SEA

there’s a small house
atop a cliff
by the sea

they’re happy there
the house provides for them
food, water, heating, books, toys

there’s a known secret in the house
only the youngest is unaware
of the condition of their livelihood

their father is unemployed
every weekday, when he goes to work
he sets them busy to something
and casts himself into the sea,
returning by the mid-afternoon,
unharmed

but, by attrition
the fear that drove him
was scraped away by the rocks,
the granite he used to rebuild himself with

they could feel it after a while,
his eyes were glassy, his face wooden
a silence permeated the house

everything they had before
was lost to the waves
