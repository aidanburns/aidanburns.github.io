1/2/2019

CURRENT YEAR

the warriors of love marched through the
nighttime streets and held their torches high.
bystanders watched from their windows with binoculars
and nested hatred for the man living across from him.
the warriors of love met their match, others
found them, their torches sparked and let out
their cries, their manifestos, thousands united
under the colours.

something was said, i have interviewed all that
were present and have not yet found what it
was, but it did not matter to them. all their
flames ran on kerosene and the street was set
alight, all fled at the threat, they fled to their
banners and proudly presented that they were
not the ones responsible for the burning homes.

nobody watching was harmed, but they did not
feel that way. the event had no real beginning
and no ending, some left through the fire
escape but most retreated up the metal stairs
to their windows. they kept the fire going from
their rooms three floors up and through steel
reinforced elevators, as they cared
passionately about their cause, buried under
mountains of ash on the dirty city streets.
