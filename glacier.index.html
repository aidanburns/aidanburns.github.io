GLACIER

you can take me apart if it’ll make me lighter
and easier to carry, cut me into ribbons
and tie them together, hoist it over your shoulders
and take it down the river,
i’ll sit at the back where it’s quiet
and i’ll keep your boat from sinking
into the water, it’s cold like winter when you’re three years old
and the wind cuts your pale face,
—you remember what happened?—
and it spun you and it twisted your neck with its forearms
and sharpened your jaw into something that
couldn’t be seen,
—i don’t want to hear—
but you stretched towards the heavens
casting shadows like burning oil
over me, scarring my collarbones,
over us, like melting red tar,
still under the raw skin on my neck
