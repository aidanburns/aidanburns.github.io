PRETTY LIGHTS

lying against the window
you see the lamps flash by
the steady rhythm of the machine

you see strips of neon
you see glimpses of the lives
of those living out there

the coach shudders and shakes
the tempo’s thrown out of sync
it roars and powers on

there’s a dimly lit convenience station
in the valley, flanked on all sides by
mountains, imposing, you’re passing through

the sky lights up; something is happening
a distant pyramid brings lights of all colours
everyone’s asleep, except for you

the pulse lives on, into the night sky
everyone wakes; you’re the sole witness
to something they’d never believe
