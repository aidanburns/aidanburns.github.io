BRIGHT BLUE

it’s at the edge of the wood
just off the motorway, before the city
barely out of sight.

it’s even got a few flags,
however much the nation the flag represents
wants nothing to do with it
it still flies, proudly
a stubborn symbol of everything
they did wrong.

there’s a rhythm to the place
the lights pan rigidly,
guards patrol in step
the same dogs wake to the
same trucks in the same cage every day.

it’s a bubble society
it’s an island nation

held onto by people
with bigger things to deal with.
can they hear the dogs?
