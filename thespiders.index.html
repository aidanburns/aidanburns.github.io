THE SPIDERS

the rain beats down on the city
from an empty sky
there is nothing to hold them back,
a dog with smiling teeth,
miles away,
miles underground,
sitting, breathing behind you, but it's not there
it's just the movement,
more people leaving in flashes of light
but when the machines break partway through,
where will the thousands go
with no bronze bull to hold them,
hollow and light, to scatter back into the square,
scurring under the eyes they built,
the chains they cast and the masks they wove,
choked by the hands of the mother they bore
