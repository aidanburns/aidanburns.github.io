CAVE PAINTINGS

the lights went on forever
the elevator came to a complete stop and he
stepped out into the tunnel.

above his head, the lights stretched upwards
into the sky, thousands of grapsing
hands with torn nails. beside him lay their
thoughts and beliefs, the bricks violently
splayed with colour. the history of the city lay
before him, he saw their hearts and minds sit
abandoned and alone.

even in death and memory, he still saw form
and it scared him. even in death and
rememberance, they were crushed under the
weight of the rock.

the bombs fell. strings of firecrackers went off
across the city and he could not believe his
eyes. the words of Prometheus crumbled like
bucket sandcastles in the path of the tide, the
bombs broke down his most devout beliefs.

he built his home where it once lay, he built a
small statue. the sun shone through the rubble
and a small gratine child reached for the sky.
