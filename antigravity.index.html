ANTIGRAVITY

there’s a box buried in your backyard
the same box everyone has,
one your parents never knew about.

it’s where your stuffed toys went
when they were too old and loved to stay together,
but they look different now
they’re black and charred.

the box sucks all the air out of the room,
the scientists wear suits with black visors
and they breathe through a tube in the wall.
there’s no weight, there’s no life inside.
it cannot tolerate them, it won’t let them breathe.
your box is here for you and you alone

this had to happen someday,
we waited on your roof as the sky turned dark
and the houses were destroyed.
one thousand explosions echoed through the city
as we all gave in to them.

i stood with you as you opened your box in your garden,
it was the dog you lost when you were nine.
you held him tightly in your arms
and then we were gone.
