ATLANTIS

if you imagine us as open,
like clear spheres on an orbital,
take a light and magnifying glass
and look inside, break it open if you have to.

the virus dragged it down, away from you
and your torch, slowly fading out into spots
as the pieces were swept away
it reached the base and was absorbed
into the object, pushing away into the sides
and surrounding the empty air.

slowly it gathered and learned,
and the fragments pulled together here,
and it built a city from the dust it found,
a great observatory. it found a truer purpose,
against its belonging, against itself,
anchoring itself to the telescope
it could only look up.
