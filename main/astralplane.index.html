ASTRAL PLANE

it was the witching hour, as
our bodies rose above our houses
and joined hands in the sky.

i saw thousands of strands
like a spiderâ€™s web,
made of rope or string, wires and arteries.

i was there, joined together with you.
knives and fishhooks bore into my chest
and bound us together,

made complete.
the net of cables and grey indistinct buzzing
sometimes spasmed with red and black

as someone curled up and was torn apart.
we held each other tightly as they slowly
stopped existing.

drifting out into space, we glimpsed a reflection
in their eye, one thousand melted together in a knot
like plastic toys.

and in our hollow centre,
a crying child
begging to be let out.
